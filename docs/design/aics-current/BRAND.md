# Allen Institute — Current Brand

**Status:** Active. Use for all new projects. Updated from Allen Institute Brand Cheat Sheet (2025).

## Identity

The Allen Institute. Foundational scientific research — brain science, cell science, immunology, and more. Audience: researchers, scientists, internal staff, and the broader public.

Brand principle: **"We are what we say and how we say it."** Identity is defined by voice — consistent as it flexes across audiences, intentions, and touchpoints.

## Design Philosophy

Bold, dynamic, and scientific. The brand combines precision with vitality. Color is used with energy and confidence — primary colors as backgrounds, accents as highlights. The forward slash `/` is a core visual motif representing connection, separation, and the breadcrumb journey of discovery.

Everything is a journey. Structure is formed of dynamic connections.

## Visual Language

### Forward Slash `/`
The forward slash articulates connection and separation, indicating more content to follow. Used in naming conventions (breadcrumb hierarchy), navigation, and typographic emphasis:
- `allen institute/brain science/cell types`
- `allen institute/search`
- `about / people / research / open science / careers`

### Logotype and Icons
- **Logotype:** `allen institute/` — lowercase, always with trailing forward slash
- **Open icon:** Reductive expression — the `a` and `/` from the logotype. Used rarely, for formal or refined applications, generally in neutral colors.
- **Lens icon:** Zooms in on the open icon. Used as the profile icon. Can contain images or video (not portraits). Can bleed off right or top edges up to 25%. Can be applied in any core palette color.
- Logotype applied horizontally or vertically. Stacked version used only when space is limited.

### Iconography
Simple, understandable, linear, geometric, and scalable.

### Image Direction
- Images feature people, science, and place — always cropped and positioned dynamically.
- Image treatment: **luminance** or **colorization** for graphic consistency. Contrast is always increased.
- Infographics are simple, dynamic, and engaging.
- Gradient backgrounds used for reversed-out headlines.

## Typography

Custom exclusive typeface: **Allen Institute** (not available on Google Fonts — must be licensed/loaded from brand assets).

Two editions:
- **Headline:** For copy >24pt. Tight letter spacing, high stroke contrast, personality-forward.
- **Text:** For copy <24pt. Wider letter spacing (never alter), curved tail `l`, lower stroke contrast for legibility.

Five weights in both Roman and Italic: Light, Regular, Medium, Bold, (Bold Italic).

Typographic hierarchy:
- `section/` — large, bold, structural label
- `headline bold` — primary heading
- `semi-bold sub-head` — secondary heading
- `articulated text/!/?@+(*)` — body with punctuation emphasis
- `italic / numbers` — supporting styles

## Color Palette Structure

Three tiers: **base** (neutrals for backgrounds and structure) → **primary** (main brand colors, used as backgrounds) → **accent** (highlights, used sparingly for energy and dynamism).

- Typography: Allen Institute custom font replaces Open Sans/Raleway
- Color: New vibrant palette replaces conservative navy/gray scheme
- No single dominant primary color — palette is multi-primary
- Bold, expressive use of color vs. the previous minimal/structural approach
